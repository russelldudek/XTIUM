Illustrative 120-Day Entry Plan
Proposed AI service operations and operating integration mandate

Operating posture: Listen before prescribing. Preserve what the current system protects. Baseline before targeting. Treat every company-specific idea as a hypothesis until validated with XTIUM leaders and frontline teams.

Days 0-30 - Read the service reality

  • Map service promises, platforms, operating variants, regional differences, customer segments and current integration decisions.
  • Conduct listening sessions across CEO/COO, product, AI engineering, security, service desk, network operations, cloud, communications, customer success, sales and transition/onboarding.
  • Identify where one-accountable-partner positioning creates hidden handoffs, conflicting ownership or duplicated tools.
  • Baseline measurement categories by service line; do not set targets before the operating context is understood.

Outputs: operating-variant map; decision-rights map; baseline inventory; two candidate lighthouse workflows.

Days 31-60 - Define lighthouse workflows

  • Select two workflows using customer value, available evidence, cross-service relevance, adoption burden and manageable risk.
  • Complete a One Service Definition for each: promise, trigger/context, AI role, human authority, evidence, ownership/escalation.
  • Identify process/platform variation to standardize, bound, adapt or retain.
  • Agree evaluation, security, rollback, customer-communication and review requirements.

Outputs: two service definitions; exception taxonomy; measurement plan; governance and review cadence.

Days 61-90 - Prove internally

  • Run the definitions inside XTIUM with frontline participation and explicit human authority.
  • Observe actual exceptions, workarounds, override patterns, failure modes and customer-experience consequences.
  • Measure speed, quality, economics, trust and adoption using disclosed baselines and units.
  • Revise standard work, training, escalation, telemetry and ownership based on evidence.

Outputs: internal operating proof; evidence review; improved standard work; transfer-readiness decision.

Days 91-120 - Package for customer deployment

  • Translate each internal proof into a customer deployment playbook with prerequisites, assumptions, adaptation points, controls and owner map.
  • Create sales-to-delivery language that distinguishes platform capability from the managed operating outcome.
  • Design customer adoption, enablement and outcome-review assets.
  • Establish a portfolio cadence to scale, adapt, hold or stop patterns and to harvest learning across customers.

Outputs: deployment playbooks; customer evidence plan; adoption package; portfolio-review cadence.

Decision gates

Scale

Repeatable outcome, acceptable risk, clear ownership, credible economics and low adaptation burden.

Adapt

Core pattern is sound, but industry, customer or regional context requires explicit variation.

Hold

Value may exist, but evidence, authority, security, ownership or adoption is incomplete.

Stop

The workflow does not create sufficient customer value, trust or operating leverage.

120-day success evidence

Not a promise of specific improvements. Success would mean leadership can see: where integration matters, who decides, what AI may do, what humans retain, how outcomes are measured, which patterns are reusable and what should not scale.
