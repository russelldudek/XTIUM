Executive Thesis Brief
AI service operations and post-merger operating integration

Pittsburgh, PA - actively relocating to Tampa Bay | 412.287.8640 | russelldudek@gmail.com | linkedin.com/in/russelldudek

Mandate hypothesis: Create one measurable operating system that turns XTIUM's combined platforms, teams and AI capabilities into repeatable managed-service outcomes - first internally, then in customer environments.

Company moment

  • ATSG and Evolve IP have been unified under XTIUM, creating a global full-stack MSP with cloud, workplace, network, communications, security, service desk and AI capabilities.
  • Leadership has stated that 2026 is a growth year and that internal AI capabilities will be packaged for customers.
  • XTAI, MPaaS, OPTX, Enterprise Service Desk and the Talkdesk partnership create a broad operating surface where platform, workflow and customer-experience integration matter.

Central tensions

Integration / continuity

Create common operating mechanisms without disrupting customer relationships or useful local practices.

Automation / trust

Increase speed and consistency while preserving security, evidence, escalation and human accountability.

Standardization / variation

Reduce platform and process duplication while adapting to service lines, industries, regions and customer environments.

Internal leverage / customer offering

Turn internal AI wins into governed deployment patterns, not demonstrations that depend on insider context.

Candidate thesis

Run it inside. Make it repeatable outside. Every AI-enabled service improvement should leave behind a clear customer promise, trigger/context definition, automation boundary, human authority, evidence standard, ownership model and adaptation guide.

One Service Definition

The operating artifact is a reusable service specification with six fields: customer promise; trigger and context; AI role; human authority; evidence standard; ownership and escalation. It is validated through two runs: internal operational proof and customer deployment translation.

Competitive landscape

  • Global integrators: Kyndryl and NTT DATA bring scale, alliances and end-to-end AI delivery. XTIUM's counter-position is greater operating intimacy and faster transfer from proof to managed outcome.
  • Vertical specialists: ECI demonstrates the power of governed AI wrapped in specific industry context. XTIUM needs explicit vertical and workflow deployment patterns around its shared platform.
  • AI-native aggregators: Thrive Holdings' model shows that investors view centralized AI operating leverage as a consolidation thesis for IT services.
  • Platform encroachment: ServiceNow and MSP software vendors continue to automate core service workflows. XTIUM must own the outcome, adoption, exceptions and customer trust - not only the tooling.

Proposed scorecard

Customer and operations

Customer effort, satisfaction, first-contact resolution, reopen/escalation, time to acknowledge/resolve, handoff count, recurring incidents and automation override.

Economics

Cost to serve, labor hours avoided, rework, tooling duplication and margin contribution by reusable pattern.

Trust and integration

Security/control exceptions, decision traceability, human-review agreement, rollback/recovery, common-workflow adoption, variants retired, pattern reuse and internal proof-to-offer latency.

All measures are discovery categories. No XTIUM baseline or target is asserted.

Evidence map

  • Vape-Jet: current AI-first operating transformation across support, customer success, engineering, ERP, telemetry and standard work.
  • Amazon: 24/7 customer operations, standard work, escalation and operating visibility.
  • Compunetics: high-reliability technical delivery, quality systems, engineering/manufacturing integration and customer consequence.
  • DudeWorth: agentic workflows, human authority, evaluation, governance, adoption and time-to-value.

Hard objection

Russell has no direct global-MSP executive tenure. The honest response is bounded transferability and a diagnostic first step: test his ability to map a service workflow, expose decision rights and evidence, and produce a practical integration/deployment mechanism.

Executive questions

  1. Which internal AI-enabled workflow is closest to becoming a customer offer, and what blocks the transfer?
  2. Where is standardization creating leverage, and where would it damage a legitimate service or customer difference?
  3. How are AI-ready service decisions split among product, operations, security and customer leadership?
  4. Which measures would prove that automation is improving customer outcomes and economics?
  5. Where does one-accountable-partner positioning create the greatest hidden integration burden?
  6. What is the strongest reason not to create a dedicated mandate at this intersection?

Conversation objective

Earn a strategic operating conversation with Michael Pisterzi and relevant XTIUM leaders. Do not frame the discussion as an application to an unverified opening. The desired next step is a bounded discovery session or workflow diagnostic that can create value whether or not a formal role emerges.
