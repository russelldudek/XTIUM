## Visible company identity
The official XTIUM wordmark appears above the fold and across all document routes, paired with clear independent-candidate language.

## Color token provenance
- `#11B7B3` - source-sampled approximation of the official teal mark.

Exact published brand tokens were not located. Teal/orange/black values are documented as source-sampled approximations from the official press logo and current public pages, not claimed as official brand-guide values.

## Typography evidence and decision
The public identity uses clean, geometric, modern sans-serif typography. No public font-license package was located. The campaign uses Helvetica Neue / Aptos / Arial fallbacks and does not copy proprietary font files.

## Photography and interface character
XTIUM's current public presentation emphasizes light fields, black typography, teal/orange recognition, clean technical geometry, managed-service diagrams and direct outcome language. The campaign avoids generic stock imagery and uses an original dual-registration service-definition composition.

## Role-derived visual translation
- Signature scene: two operating plates - internal XTIUM proof and customer deployment - align around One Service Definition.
- Motion: the two plates move into registration; motion is disabled under reduced-motion preferences.
- Interaction: service scenarios change the promise, context, AI role, human authority, evidence and ownership readouts.
- Independent distinction: every surface states that this is Russell Dudek's independent candidate vision and not an official XTIUM publication.

## Anti-clone decision
The campaign does not reuse Delivery Weave, dispatch board, proving ground, proof ledger, constellation, orbit, control room, network graph, flywheel, sequencer, waveform or threshold-field visual systems. The dual-run service-definition registration is derived from XTIUM's internal-AI-to-customer-service challenge.
