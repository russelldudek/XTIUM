Michael,

XTIUM's public story is unusually compelling: a unified global managed-services company, a security-first full-stack portfolio, AI already operating inside the business, and an explicit ambition to package those capabilities for customers. The operating challenge underneath that story is equally significant. A merger can combine organizations and offerings; it does not automatically create one repeatable way to define, run, measure and transfer AI-enabled services.

My background sits in that operating layer. At Amazon, I built mechanisms for 24/7 customer-backward operations: standard work, visibility, escalation, labor planning and continuous improvement. At Compunetics, I led engineering, manufacturing, quality and customer delivery in high-reliability technical environments. Today at Vape-Jet, I lead AI-first operating transformation across support, customer success, engineering issue flow, ERP, telemetry, standard work and a software-enabled machine fleet. Through DudeWorth, I develop agentic workflow patterns that connect AI capability to human authority, evaluation, governance, adoption and measurable operating outcomes.

I have not led operations inside a global MSP, and I would not pretend otherwise. The adjacency is the point: I have repeatedly entered complex technical systems, made the work and decision rights visible, standardized what should be common, protected necessary variation, and built mechanisms that turn emerging technology into adopted work.

I created the independent candidate vision at https://russelldudek.github.io/XTIUM/ to make that thinking concrete. Its One Service Definition model explores how an internal AI-enabled workflow can become a secure, measurable customer deployment pattern without claiming knowledge of XTIUM's undisclosed processes.

Given XTIUM's Oldsmar presence and my active relocation to Tampa Bay, I would value a conversation about where integration, AI-enabled service delivery and customer deployment are creating the greatest operating leverage - or the greatest friction - in the combined company.

Carpe diem,

Russell Dudek
